Kingdom of Bhutan

Location

      Bhutan, "Land of the Thunder Dragon”, is a mountainous country, which is a landlocked country, located in Southern Asia between China and India. Nepal, located to the West of Bhutan, and Bangladesh, located to the South of Bhutan, are also neighbor countries; however, they do not share a land border with Bhutan.

Area, Population; Ethnic groups & Official Language

      The territory of Bhutan is 38,394 square kilometers or 14,824 square miles, ranks as 133rd for the land area, and has a population of over 788,615, as the 160th in population. The density of population is 19.3/km2 or alternatively, 50.0/sq mi.
      Primarily, there are the Ngalops and the Sharchops in the Bhutanese people, known as the Western Bhutanese and Eastern Bhutanese respectively. There are slightly more the Sharchops in the statistics of Bhutanese population whereas in the politics, there are more the Ngalops, as the King and the political elite, who typically live in the western part of Bhutan. They are closely related to Tibetan, who live in the Southwest China. Their official and national language is Dzongkha, which is a Sino-Tibetan language.

Climate & Seasons

      As the elevation and direction of location, the climate of Bhutan differs: temperate in the highlands, the central and eastern Bhutan; subtropical in the south; whereas in the north, there is polar-type climate, snowing all whole year. Moreover, the seasons are also five seasons in Bhutan: summer, monsoon, autumn, winter and spring, which is unlike with most countries.

Authority [Monarch & Prime minister]

      Jigme Khesar Namgyel Wangchuck is Druk Gyalpo or ‘the Dragon King’ of the Kingdom of Bhutan. His spouse is Jetsun Pema, who is Druk Gyaltsuen or ‘the Dragon Queen’ of Bhutan, is also the mother of Prince Jigme Namgyel Wangchuck and Jigme Ugyen Wangchuck, the second child.


      Prime Minister is Lotay Tshering who is also the president of Druk Nyamrup Tshogpa, which is the Social Democratic Party in Bhutan. His spouse is Ugyen Dema.

Districts, Provinces & Capital city

      There are 20 districts or ‘dzongkhags’ in Bhutan:
Bumthang, Chhukha, Dagana, Gasa, Haa, Lhuentse, Mongar, Paro, Pema Gatshel, Punakha, Samdrup Jongkhar, Samtse, Sarpang, Thimphu, Trashigang, Trashi Yangtse, Trongsa, Tsirang, Wangdue Phodrang, and Zhemgang
      There are 9 provinces in Bhutan:
Trongsa, Paro, Punakha, Wangdue Phodrang, Daga, Bumthang, Thimphu, Kurtoed, and Kurmaed.
However, as Kurtoed and Kurmaed were combined into one local administration, it could be also counted as 8 provinces.

      Thimphu, the largest city in Bhutan, is the Capitcal of Bhutan after it has replaced Punakha, the ancient capital city of Bhutan. It is situated in the western central part of Bhutan, which is surrounded by the valley of the Thimphu District.

Culture & Religion


      Starting from the monarch to farmers, the citizens of Bhutan are said to be afraid of the possible influences by the outside that would affect the heritages, traditions and culture of Bhutan. Therefore, even the institution with some strict policies is made to preserve and sustain their heritages, traditions and culture identities. Therefore, they are referred as ‘The Last Shangri-La’ for their unspoiled traditional and cultural heritages. They value not only their culture but also the religion, and hence they are known as devoted and religious. In Bhutan, the Vajrayana Buddhism is regarded as the state religion, as most of the traditions of Bhutan derivate from Buddhism. The second most dominant religion is Hinduism, which is also widely spread in the southern regions. There are also other religions but the percentages of the devoted are low.
